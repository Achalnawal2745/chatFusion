Automata Theory deals with the study of abstract machines and the computational problems that can be solved using them. It forms the backbone of modern computer science, providing the mathematical framework for understanding what algorithms can and cannot do.

The core concepts revolve around the relationship between Languages, Grammars, and Automata. These are categorized in the Chomsky Hierarchy, ranging from Finite State Machines to the universal power of Turing Machines.
